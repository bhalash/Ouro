3D Bioprinting


                    

                        The field of 3D printing is today one of the most dynamic areas of manufacturing technologies. Hobbyists and professional manufacturies alike can conceptualize a three-dimensional creation, diagram it with CAD software, and bring it into reality then and there from their desk. Many exotic and mundance materials have been used in 3D printers over time; plastic, paper, ice, molten metal, gelatin and even liquid chocolate. 3D bioprinting is the is the evolutionary next step in this line: Spatially-controlled cell patterns; muscles, bones, skin and eventually even whole organs printed layer by layer from a seed of cloned stem cells. 
                    

                    

                        We have developed the Ouro Bioprinter – a scalable technology platform to fabricate reproducible organ-specific biocompatible 3D biopolymer hydrogels (alginate, collagen, chitosan, fibrin). The Ouro Bioprinter will enable you to create stem cell niches for stem cell biology and has broad implications for designing better drug screening models, using fewer animals and developing approaches for personal medicines beyond genomic.
