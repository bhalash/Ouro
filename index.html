Bioprinting

Hobbyist and professional workshops alike can conceptualize a three-dimensional object, diagram it in CAD software and bring it into reality then and there.

3D bioprinting is the evolutionary next step in this line of technology: spatially-controlled cell patterns printed layer by layer in arbitrary arrangements. Bioprinting can print both soft and hard tissues; muscles, nerves, bones, skin and eventually entire complex organs might eventually be printed from a laboratory bench.

The bioprinting process can follow one of several paths, given the differentiation between cells in different types of the body. Generally, the tissue is extruded in successive layers into a nutritious substrate through direct cell extrusion through a controlled syringe. The extruded material becomes the scaffold around which the targeted tissue grows.

Recent breakthroughs in this field include blood vessels, heart wall tissue and skin that might eventually be transplantable.

One of the key benefits of bioprinted tissue is that it can be grown from donor stem cells to thus reduce or even eliminate the chance of rejection or a lifelong dependence on immunosuppressive drugs.

Ouro: Making it Better

With the Ouro, we have developed the next generation platform for tissue engineering and regenerative medical technologies. Our platform boasts modularity, greater reliability and improved resolution compared to existing bioprinters.

The Ouro bioprinter offers a scalable tool for fabrication of organ-specific 3D biopolymer hydrogels with controlled precision. It will enable you to create targeted stem cell niches and has implications that extend all the way across the field of 3D tissue engineering: more effective drug screening models, fewer animal tests and new paths for personal medicines beyond genomic.

We designed the Ouro as user-friendly platform, offering a fine degree of control directly from your desktop. Ouro operations can be paused, tweaked and resumed with ease. It is network capable and compatible with a wide range of industry standard design and control software.

We are dedicated to making bioprinting an accessible and affordable reality for those who need it. We believe that the Ouro is at the forefront of next generation tissue engineering and offers a revolutionary tool for biotechnology stakeholders.
