Bioprinting

The field of 3D printing is today one of the most dynamic areas of manufacturing technologies. Hobbyists and professional manufacturies alike can conceptualize a three-dimensional creation, diagram it with CAD software, and bring it into reality then and there from their desk. Many exotic and mundance materials have been used in 3D printers over time; plastic, paper, ice, molten metal, gelatin and even liquid chocolate.

3D bioprinting is the is the evolutionary next step in this line: Spatially-controlled cell patterns; muscles, bones, skin and eventually even whole organs printed layer by layer from a seed of cloned stem cells.
